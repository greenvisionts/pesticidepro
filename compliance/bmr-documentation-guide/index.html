What is a Batch Manufacturing Record (BMR)?

A Batch Manufacturing Record is a complete written record of every step taken during the manufacture of a specific batch of pesticide or agrochemical. The Insecticides Rules 1971 require pesticide manufacturers to maintain batch-wise manufacturing records and make them available for inspection by CIB officials and state drug controllers.

BMR Retention Requirements Under Insecticides Rules

Batch Manufacturing Records must be retained for a minimum of 3 years from the date of manufacture, or 1 year after the expiry date of the product, whichever is longer. CIB inspectors can request records for any batch produced during this period. Electronic records must remain accessible and readable throughout the retention period.

Common BMR Errors Found in CIB Inspections

The most frequent BMR findings during CIB inspections: (1) Blank spaces — entries missing without N/A justification. (2) Correction fluid used — Tipp-Ex or similar whitener on any entry. (3) Backdated signatures — signing multiple steps at once. (4) Missing in-process test results. (5) Yield not calculated or outside acceptable range without investigation. (6) Raw material lot numbers missing — untraceable inputs. (7) QA release signature missing or added after batch was already dispatched.

Can a batch be released without a complete BMR?
No. Under Insecticides Rules 1971, every batch must have a complete and approved BMR before product can be dispatched for sale. Releasing a batch with an incomplete BMR is a violation of manufacturing licence conditions and can result in licence suspension upon CIB inspection.
What happens if a CIB inspector finds BMR errors?
Minor BMR deficiencies result in a warning letter with timeline for correction. Serious deficiencies (missing records, falsified records, multiple incomplete batches) result in show-cause notice, potential manufacturing stop-work order, and enhanced surveillance inspections. Repeated violations can lead to licence cancellation.
