Insecticides Act 1968 — Complete Compliance Guide India

The Insecticides Act 1968 and Insecticides Rules 1971 govern the entire pesticide industry in India. Complete guide for manufacturers, importers, and distributors.

Try CIB Compliance Software →

Scope and Applicability

Covers: insecticides, fungicides, herbicides, rodenticides, nematicides, acaricides, and bio-pesticides. Agricultural, public health, and industrial pesticides all included. Governs manufacturing, import, export, sale, transport, distribution, and use.

Prohibition and Restrictions

Section 27 empowers Government to ban/restrict pesticides. As of 2024, 66 pesticides are banned in India. Schedule under Insecticides Rules lists banned (Part A) and restricted (Part B) pesticides. Manufacturers must monitor and stop production/sale of newly banned molecules immediately.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Manufacturing/importing/selling without licence or registration: imprisonment up to 2 years or fine up to ₹5,000 or both. Manufacture below specification: imprisonment up to 2 years. Misrepresentation in label: imprisonment up to 1 year. Failure to maintain records: fine up to ₹500/day.

Registration Committee Functions

RC reviews: product registration applications, efficacy data, toxicology data, residue data. Grants provisional (9.3) and regular (9.4) registrations. Prescribes label requirements for each registration. Sets maximum residue limits (MRL). Can revoke registrations for safety reasons.

2024 Regulatory Updates

Pesticides Management Bill to replace Insecticides Act 1968 has been pending since 2008. Key proposed changes: stronger environmental safety, enhanced data requirements, stricter penalties, mandatory post-market surveillance. Track Parliamentary progress. Current compliance is still under 1968 Act until new law is enacted.
