What is Aphid?

Aphid (Myzus persicae / Aphis gossypii) is a major sucking pest that causes significant yield losses in Chilli / Capsicum across India and globally. The pest causes damage by phloem sap sucking.

In India, Aphid in Chilli / Capsicum is a serious problem in Andhra, Telangana, Karnataka. Left uncontrolled, it can cause 30-70% yield loss in severe infestations.

How to Identify Aphid in Chilli / Capsicum

🔍 Key Symptoms on Chilli / Capsicum:

curled yellow leaves, sticky honeydew, sooty mold

Damage Pattern

The Aphid is a sucking pest that damages Chilli / Capsicum through phloem sap sucking. Damage is most severe during Kharif & Rabi season when conditions favour pest multiplication.

Spray Schedule for Aphid in Chilli / Capsicum

When to Spray

Monitor your Chilli / Capsicum crop regularly. Spray when:

  • Economic Threshold Level (ETL) reached: 5-10 aphid per leaf or shoot
  • First visible damage symptoms appear
  • During peak pest season for Chilli / Capsicum in your region
💡 Expert Tip: Spray in the early morning (before 9 AM) or late evening (after 5 PM) for best results. Avoid spraying during peak sunlight hours or when wind speed is above 8 km/h.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best insecticide for Aphid in Chilli / Capsicum?

Imidacloprid 17.8% SL is highly effective. Apply at 0.25-0.3 ml/litre as a spray solution ensuring thorough leaf coverage. For severe infestations, use Thiamethoxam 25% WG as rotation. PHI: 3-4 days.

How to identify Aphid damage in Chilli / Capsicum?

Look for: curled yellow leaves, sticky honeydew, sooty mold. Use a magnifying glass to spot the pest on leaf undersides. Compare with a Aphid identification chart.

When to spray for Aphid in Chilli / Capsicum?

Spray when infestation reaches ETL (Economic Threshold Level). For Chilli / Capsicum, this is typically 5-10 aphid per leaf. During peak season (Kharif & Rabi), monitor every 3-4 days.

Is Aphid spreading to other crops near Chilli / Capsicum?

Yes. Aphid can spread to neighbouring crops. If your Chilli / Capsicum is near Tomato, Cotton, Rice / Paddy, monitor those crops too. Create buffer zones with sticky traps to monitor spread.
