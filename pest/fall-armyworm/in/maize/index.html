What is Fall Armyworm?

Fall Armyworm (Spodoptera frugiperda) is a major defoliator that causes significant yield losses in Maize / Corn across India and globally. The pest causes damage by larval feeding on leaves and growing point.

In India, Fall Armyworm in Maize / Corn is a serious problem in Karnataka, Andhra, Maharashtra. Left uncontrolled, it can cause 30-70% yield loss in severe infestations.

How to Identify Fall Armyworm in Maize / Corn

🔍 Key Symptoms on Maize / Corn:

windowpane feeding, frass in whorl, growing point destruction

Damage Pattern

The Fall Armyworm is a defoliator that damages Maize / Corn through larval feeding on leaves and growing point. Damage is most severe during Kharif & Rabi season when conditions favour pest multiplication.

Spray Schedule for Fall Armyworm in Maize / Corn

When to Spray

Monitor your Maize / Corn crop regularly. Spray when:

  • Economic Threshold Level (ETL) reached: 5-10 fall armyworm per leaf or shoot
  • First visible damage symptoms appear
  • During peak pest season for Maize / Corn in your region
💡 Expert Tip: Spray in the early morning (before 9 AM) or late evening (after 5 PM) for best results. Avoid spraying during peak sunlight hours or when wind speed is above 8 km/h.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best insecticide for Fall Armyworm in Maize / Corn?

Emamectin Benzoate 5% SG is highly effective. Apply at 0.25-0.5 g/litre as a spray solution ensuring thorough leaf coverage. For severe infestations, use Chlorantraniliprole 18.5% SC as rotation. PHI: 5-7 days.

How to identify Fall Armyworm damage in Maize / Corn?

Look for: windowpane feeding, frass in whorl, growing point destruction. Use a magnifying glass to spot the pest on leaf undersides. Compare with a Fall Armyworm identification chart.

When to spray for Fall Armyworm in Maize / Corn?

Spray when infestation reaches ETL (Economic Threshold Level). For Maize / Corn, this is typically 5-10 fall armyworm per leaf. During peak season (Kharif & Rabi), monitor every 3-4 days.

Is Fall Armyworm spreading to other crops near Maize / Corn?

Yes. Fall Armyworm can spread to neighbouring crops. If your Maize / Corn is near Tomato, Cotton, Rice / Paddy, monitor those crops too. Create buffer zones with sticky traps to monitor spread.
