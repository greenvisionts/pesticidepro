🐛 Rust Disease Management Guide — India

Puccinia spp. (fungal pathogen) — affects Wheat, soybean, groundnut, coffee, bean, sugarcane. This guide covers identification, damage symptoms, and chemical control schedule for Indian conditions.

ParameterDetails
Scientific Name / GroupPuccinia spp. (fungal pathogen)
Affected CropsWheat, soybean, groundnut, coffee, bean, sugarcane
Peak Damage PeriodCrop-dependent; monitor from planting/bud break onwards

Identification and Damage Symptoms

Orange-yellow pustules on leaf undersides (urediniospores), premature leaf drop, loss of photosynthetic area, yield reduction

Recommended Chemical Control

Propiconazole 25EC @ 1 mL/L, Tebuconazole 25.9EC @ 1 mL/L, Mancozeb 75WP @ 2.5 g/L + Carbendazim 50WP @ 1g/L

Management Tips

Apply protectant fungicide before disease onset in susceptible varieties, monitor weather (wet conditions favour rust), 2–3 applications at 10–14 day intervals

Integrated Pest Management (IPM)

For sustainable control: (1) Regular scouting at least twice weekly. (2) Economic threshold levels (ETL) guide spray timing — do not spray below ETL. (3) Use pheromone traps to monitor population before damage threshold. (4) Preserve natural enemies — avoid broad-spectrum chemicals when natural enemy populations are high. (5) Rotate chemical groups to prevent resistance development.
