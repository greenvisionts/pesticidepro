What is Thrips?

Thrips (Thrips tabaci / Scirtothrips dorsalis) is a major sucking pest that causes significant yield losses in Okra / Bhindi across India and globally. The pest causes damage by rasping and sucking.

In India, Thrips in Okra / Bhindi is a serious problem in all India. Left uncontrolled, it can cause 30-70% yield loss in severe infestations.

How to Identify Thrips in Okra / Bhindi

🔍 Key Symptoms on Okra / Bhindi:

silver streaks on leaves, distorted growing points

Damage Pattern

The Thrips is a sucking pest that damages Okra / Bhindi through rasping and sucking. Damage is most severe during Kharif & Rabi season when conditions favour pest multiplication.

Spray Schedule for Thrips in Okra / Bhindi

When to Spray

Monitor your Okra / Bhindi crop regularly. Spray when:

  • Economic Threshold Level (ETL) reached: 5-10 thrips per leaf or shoot
  • First visible damage symptoms appear
  • During peak pest season for Okra / Bhindi in your region
💡 Expert Tip: Spray in the early morning (before 9 AM) or late evening (after 5 PM) for best results. Avoid spraying during peak sunlight hours or when wind speed is above 8 km/h.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is the best insecticide for Thrips in Okra / Bhindi?

Imidacloprid 17.8% SL is highly effective. Apply at 0.25-0.5 ml/litre as a spray solution ensuring thorough leaf coverage. For severe infestations, use Spinosad 45% SC as rotation. PHI: 3-4 days.

How to identify Thrips damage in Okra / Bhindi?

Look for: silver streaks on leaves, distorted growing points. Use a magnifying glass to spot the pest on leaf undersides. Compare with a Thrips identification chart.

When to spray for Thrips in Okra / Bhindi?

Spray when infestation reaches ETL (Economic Threshold Level). For Okra / Bhindi, this is typically 5-10 thrips per leaf. During peak season (Kharif & Rabi), monitor every 3-4 days.

Is Thrips spreading to other crops near Okra / Bhindi?

Yes. Thrips can spread to neighbouring crops. If your Okra / Bhindi is near Tomato, Cotton, Rice / Paddy, monitor those crops too. Create buffer zones with sticky traps to monitor spread.
